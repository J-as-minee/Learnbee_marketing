# Course A: Fixed vs. Growth: Understanding Your Mindset

**Tagline:** What you believe about your brain changes everything.
**Audience:** All employees / general learners
**Objective:** Understand the difference between fixed and growth mindsets, recognise your own fixed-mindset triggers, and take the first steps toward thinking like a learner.
**Duration:** ~15 minutes
**Language:** English

## Module 1: The Two Mindsets

### Slide 1 — Title Slide
**Title:** Fixed vs. Growth: Understanding Your Mindset
**Tagline:** What you believe about your brain changes everything.

---

### Slide 2 — Big Statement
Your abilities aren't fixed. They're a starting point.

---

### Slide 3 — Image + Content (Left): Carol Dweck's Discovery
**Title:** The Research That Changed Everything
**Content:**
Psychologist Carol Dweck spent decades studying why some people bounce back from failure while others give up. She ran studies with thousands of students — and the answer wasn't IQ or talent. It came down to what people believed about their own intelligence.

People who believed their abilities were fixed gave up faster, avoided hard tasks, and felt crushed by criticism. People who believed abilities could grow did the opposite — they leaned in. Dweck called this the difference between a **fixed mindset** and a **growth mindset**.

---

### Slide 4 — Key Points: The Two Mindsets Defined
**Title:** Two Ways of Seeing Yourself

- **Fixed Mindset:** Believes intelligence and talent are fixed traits you're born with. Avoids challenges to protect self-image. Gives up when things get hard. Sees effort as a sign of low ability. Feels threatened by others' success.
- **Growth Mindset:** Believes abilities can be developed through effort, learning, and good strategies. Embraces challenges as opportunities. Persists through setbacks. Sees effort as the path to mastery. Finds lessons in others' success.
- **The key insight:** Neither mindset is permanent. Most people have both — depending on the situation. The goal is to notice when the fixed voice shows up.

---

### Slide 5 — Quiz MCQ: Check Your Understanding
**Question:** Which of these reflects a growth mindset?
- A) "I'm just not a numbers person." 
- B) "This is hard, but I can figure it out with practice." ✅
- C) "Natural talent is what separates the best from the rest."
- D) "If I can't do it easily, I'm probably not cut out for it."

**Explanation:** Growth mindset is about believing that ability develops — not that everything comes easy or that you'll succeed at everything.

## Module 2: Spotting the Mindset in You

### Slide 6 — Big Statement
The fixed mindset isn't a character flaw. It's a voice everyone has.

---

### Slide 7 — Accordion: Fixed Mindset in Everyday Moments
**Title:** Recognise the Voice

**Item 1 — At Work:**
Fixed voice: "My manager didn't give me that project because I'm not leadership material."
Growth reframe: "I can ask for feedback on what to develop and build toward that opportunity."

**Item 2 — After Feedback:**
Fixed voice: "They're criticising me. They don't rate me."
Growth reframe: "This is information I can actually use. What specifically can I do differently?"

**Item 3 — Learning Something New:**
Fixed voice: "I should already know how to do this."
Growth reframe: "Not knowing it yet is exactly why I'm here learning."

**Item 4 — When Others Succeed:**
Fixed voice: "They just got lucky. Or they had better connections."
Growth reframe: "What did they do that I could learn from?"

---

### Slide 8 — Scenario Challenge: The Promotion
**Scenario:** You've been working hard for a promotion. Your colleague — who you feel is less experienced — gets it instead of you. What's your first instinct?

- **Option A:** "The system is unfair. There's nothing I can do." — *This is the fixed voice. It removes your agency entirely.*
- **Option B:** "I wonder what they demonstrated that I haven't yet. I'll ask for feedback." ✅ — *Growth mindset. Uncomfortable, but useful.*
- **Option C:** "They must have just been more liked by management." — *This is also fixed — it attributes their success to luck, not effort.*

## Module 3: The Brain That Can Change

### Slide 10 — Big Statement
Your brain physically changes every time you learn something new. Scientists call it neuroplasticity. It means the fixed mindset is wrong about one fundamental thing: you're never done growing.

---

### Slide 11 — Key Points: What Growth Mindset Actually Looks Like
**Title:** Clearing Up the Misconceptions

- **It's not about positivity:** Growth mindset isn't "think positive." It's about how you respond to difficulty — with curiosity instead of avoidance.
- **Effort is the path, not the destination:** The goal isn't to try harder at the same thing. It's to learn better strategies and keep iterating.
- **One word changes everything:** Adding "yet" to your thinking — "I can't do this... yet" — is small, but it shifts your brain toward possibility.
- **Small moments compound:** How you talk to yourself after a mistake, a setback, or a criticism shapes your mindset over time. Awareness is the first step.

---

### Slide 12 — Quiz MCQ: Final Check
**Question:** A growth mindset means...
- A) Believing you can do anything if you just try hard enough.
- B) Staying positive and optimistic no matter what happens.
- C) Believing your abilities can develop through learning, effort, and good strategies. ✅
- D) Never doubting yourself or feeling uncertain.

**Explanation:** Growth mindset is grounded in evidence — your brain changes with learning. It's not about confidence or positivity. It's about knowing that effort and strategy lead to growth.
